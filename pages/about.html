PHD Family Welfare Foundation

Registered Trust • 80G • FCRA

The PHD Family Welfare Foundation (PHDFWF) is a registered non-profit organisation promoted by PHDCCI and registered under the Indian Trust Act, 1882, 80G of the IT Act, 1961 and the FCRA. The Foundation has primarily concentrated on implementing integrated health and family welfare projects, skill development, mobile medical care, support for persons with disabilities, educational courses and women empowerment initiatives across India.

PHDFWF currently operates 12 Mobile Medical Vans across Delhi NCR, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Haryana and Gujarat, serving approximately 1,100 beneficiaries daily. The Foundation also runs 34 Skill Development Centres across multiple states and tribal regions, benefiting thousands of youth every year. The main focus areas of the Foundation include: health care, skill development training, education, support & care for specially-abled people, sanitation facilities for girls in schools, rehabilitation & relief during natural calamities, and self-defence training for women. The foundation also supports 100 orphanages and several old age homes in Delhi NCR by providing free health care and basic needs.

Our Mission

Empowering Communities Through Sustainable Development

To empower underserved communities through healthcare, education, skill development, women empowerment and inclusive social welfare initiatives, creating sustainable opportunities for self-reliance and long-term community development across India.

Our Vision

Building An Inclusive And Self-Reliant Society

To create an inclusive and empowered society where every individual, especially women, youth and underprivileged communities, has equal access to healthcare, education, skill development and opportunities for a dignified and self-reliant life.

Anuradha Goel

Leadership Message

Chairperson's Message

"For over four decades, our mission has been to create meaningful and sustainable change by improving access to healthcare, education, skill development and social welfare for underserved communities across India."

Anuradha Goel is an active philanthropist involved in social welfare activities for the last 40 years. She currently serves as the Chairperson of the PHD Family Welfare Foundation and the PHD Chamber's Women and Child Development Committee, leading programmes focused on healthcare, health awareness, water conservation, skill development and income generation.

She is the President and founding member of St. Stephen's Hospital Patients Welfare Society and has been instrumental in raising funds and facilitating treatment for more than 12,000 children. She has also led educational and income generation programmes for women from resettlement areas for over two decades.

Ms. Goel is a member of the Delhi Social Welfare Board, Vice Chairperson of SCWEC India and serves on the governing bodies of Shriram Bharatiya Kala Kendra and Shriram Centre for Performing Arts.

PHD Family Welfare Foundation is a registered trust working towards healthcare, education, skill development and social welfare initiatives across India since 1985.
